PhD Research @ UIUC
EMI in Wide-Bandgap Motor Drives & Electrified Propulsion

I study how modern SiC/GaN-based motor drives interact with machines and power systems from an EMI/EMC perspective. This includes modeling conducted emissions, understanding parasitic elements, and exploring mitigation strategies relevant for future electric aircraft and high-power-density propulsion.

Keywords: EMI/EMC, LISN measurements, dv/dt filters, wide-bandgap devices, electric propulsion.

Projects

A few things I’ve built or worked on that I keep coming back to.

AI-driven Microgrid Scheduling
Genetic Algorithm + ML Forecasting · University of Moratuwa

Developed an AI-assisted day-ahead optimal scheduling framework for a campus microgrid using LightGBM-based solar/load forecasting and a tailored genetic algorithm. The proposed dispatch strategy reduced electricity costs while accounting for battery degradation.

Electric Vehicle Routing for Last-mile Delivery
Genetic Algorithm · Sri Lankan Urban Context

Modeled and optimized low-range EV fleets for grocery delivery, exploring feasibility and routing strategies under local constraints and infrastructure realities.

Optimal Power Flow with Graph Neural Networks
IEEE 30 & 130 Bus Systems

Implemented a GNN-based model to approximate AC optimal power flow solutions, aiming to reduce computation time while respecting system constraints.
